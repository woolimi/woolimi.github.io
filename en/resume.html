About

Hello, I am Woolim Park, a developer.

While studying mechanical engineering at Inha University in my third year, I became interested in programming and decided to pursue this path. I was fascinated by the educational philosophy of Ecole42, a programmer training institution in Paris, France, and studied there. I worked as a backend developer intern at Ecole42 for 6 months, then as a frontend developer at Luko, an insurance startup, for 3 years. After Luko was acquired by Allianz Direct France, I worked there for a year and a half. I am currently wrapping up my life in France and looking for a new job in Korea.

I am not hesitant to learn new things.

In a world where new technologies and methodologies emerge every day, I believe that the ability to quickly learn new things is essential for web developers. I studied mechanical engineering but became a programmer, was born in Korea but worked in France, and started as a backend developer intern but became a frontend developer. I am not hesitant to challenge myself in new fields, and if it is the path I want to take, I am willing to take on the challenge.

I enjoy sharing knowledge with others.

I studied at Ecole42, a school without classes or professors, using a peer-to-peer approach covering fields from C programming to the fundamentals of AI. Finding answers on my own, sharing knowledge with others, and giving and receiving feedback in problem-solving are very familiar to me.

I respect diversity and value collaboration.

I studied and completed numerous projects with students from all over the world, and I have experience collaborating with people from across Europe as the startup I worked for in France grew and expanded its business to Spain and Germany. Being able to communicate in English or French with people of various nationalities is another strength of mine.

What is Ecole42?

Ecole 42 operates through a P2P (peer-to-peer) based self-directed learning system without professors or traditional classes, and is authorized by the French Ministry of Education to award national qualifications (RNCP). I completed 61 projects including AI and web development projects to obtain the RNCP 7 (master's level) certification. For more details, please refer to the introduction to Ecole42.
